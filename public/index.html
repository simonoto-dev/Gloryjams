The Mission
Every night is a jam night
if you know where to look.

Glory Jams started as a simple idea: musicians should play together more. Not through streaming algorithms, not through social media content funnels, not through networking events where nobody actually plays.

Through jam sessions. The oldest, most honest way musicians have found each other for a hundred years. You show up, you sit in, you play. No audition. No cover charge. No followers required.

Oakland has one of the deepest music scenes on the West Coast, but finding out where the jams are happening means knowing someone who knows someone. We're building the map so you don't need to.

"The music industry is really good at turning music into content and really bad at getting musicians in a room together. We're fixing the second one."
-- Simonoto, founder

Jam Etiquette
The unwritten rules,
written down.

Jam sessions work because musicians look out for each other. These aren't policies -- they're the common-sense code that keeps sessions fun, fair, and musical for everyone.

01

Listen More Than You Play

The best musicians at a jam are the best listeners. Leave space. Lock in with the groove. Your moment will come.

02

Respect the Sign-Up

Wait your turn. Don't jump the list. If the host says three songs, play three songs. Everybody gets a shot.

03

Support the Venue

Buy a drink. Tip the bartender. These spots give us a room for free -- the least we can do is be good customers.

04

All Levels Welcome

Don't big-time people. The kid who just started playing deserves the same respect as the 30-year vet. We all started somewhere.

05

Keep Your Volume in Check

Nobody wants to compete with a Marshall stack at a jazz jam. Read the room, match the energy, and let everyone be heard.

06

Bring Good Vibes

No drama, no ego, no gatekeeping. If someone's struggling, encourage them. If someone's killing it, let them know. This is community.
